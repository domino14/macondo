analyze-turn -turn <n>

Analyzes a single turn in the loaded game.

Options:
  -turn <n>  Turn number to analyze (0-indexed event number)

This command analyzes just one specific turn instead of the entire game,
which is much faster for debugging or examining specific positions.

The turn number is the 0-indexed event number from the game history.
Use 'list' to see all events and their numbers.

The analysis shows:
  - Player's rack and the move they played
  - The optimal move according to the analyzer
  - Win probability loss (for sim/PEG phases) or spread loss (for endgame)
  - Known opponent rack (if revealed by a challenged phony)
  - Mistake category (Small/Medium/Large)
  - Special flags (phony, missed challenge, blown endgame, missed bingo)

Example:
  analyze-turn -turn 5    # Analyze event #5

See also: analyze, analyze-batch, list
